The questions that matter

Straight answers to what owners actually ask.

No jargon. No fine print. No surprises.

What's the catch? Do you take a percentage of my revenue or ownership?
No. We take no revenue share and no equity. You keep every dollar you earn and full ownership of your practice. Portrait makes money when you order through the marketplace and use the software - the same relationship you have with any supplier. That is the only way we profit from the relationship.
Am I locked into a long-term contract?
No. Membership renews quarterly. If the economics stop working for you, you leave at the end of your quarter - no penalty, no early termination fee. We do not rely on a long-term contract to keep members. The savings are what keep people here.
Do I need to leave my current distributor?
No. You can run Portrait alongside your existing supplier from day one. Most owners start with a few of their highest-volume products, compare the pricing, and move more over as the savings prove out. There is no pressure to consolidate immediately and no minimum order requirement to stay active.
If I leave, what happens to my patient data?
Your data belongs to you. Patient records, charting history, and your patient list are all exportable at any time, at no cost. We do not charge exit fees or restrict access to data you created in the system. You own it on the way in and you own it on the way out.
Will the pricing actually be better for a practice my size?
For most independent practices that buy their own supply, yes. Portrait aggregates purchasing across 1,200+ member practices, which gives the group access to national-account pricing - the same rates a much larger chain would negotiate. The actual number depends on what you order and how much. Run your products through the calculator to see what it looks like at your volume.
Is the EHR actually complimentary?
Yes. Core EHR access is complimentary at Bronze status and above - no separate license fee and no per-seat charge. The supply savings cover it. Portrait earns when you order, so the software does not need to be a separate line item for the model to work.
How quickly can I be up and running?
Most practices are placing their first orders within 30 days. During setup you get a Customer Success Executive assigned to walk you through onboarding - marketplace access, EHR configuration, and your first orders. Because you are not replacing your existing supplier on day one, you do not need to wait for a full migration before you start seeing the savings.
